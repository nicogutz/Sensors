How to measure force?
â€¢ It can be balanced again gravitation force
â€¢ Acceleration can be measured of a known mass
â€¢ It can produce an hydraulic pressure which is measured
â€¢ It can develop a displacement on an elastic material
3
Now, how can we measure force? Force can be measured in a number of
ways:
â€¢ First, thanks to the gravity force, we can balance the force we want to
measure against the gravitation. If we know the mass and the gravity
acceleration, we can calculate the gravitational force.
â€¢ Second, we can measure force through the acceleration. If we know the
mass, according to Newtonâ€™s first law (F=ma), and we can measure the
acceleration, we can calculate the force needed to displace this mass.
â€¢ Third, some transducer can transduce force intro pressure, which we can
then measure. This is the example of both sensors shown, where the
hydraulic load cell and the pneumatic load cell convert pressure into force.
â€¢ BUT, the most used method to measure force is the force that can develop
a displacement on an elastic material. What is an elastic material? (a
spring for instance). Here you can find a very typical example, we have a
spring (spring constant k) and we have a linear variable differential
transformer to measure the displacement. This session we will dedicate it
mostly to this elastic force transducer

This is the most used force sensor, the load cell.
We have a steel bar, and the force is transduced to the compression of that
steel bar.
This compression is sensed by four strain gages (all around).
If you look closely, the strain gage 1 is positioned vertically while the strain
gage 2 is horizontal. Why do we need that? If we put the four strain gages in
the same direction and we compress.
We can put the four gages on a Wheatstone bridge. What will happen? The
effect on the four gages will be the same and we will not have an output. If
you remember, in order to have a good sensor, we needed two resistors to
increase and two to decrease. This is not possible here if we have all gages
in the same direction.
Thus, two of them are horizontal, and two are vertical. Why is this the
solution? If we put a force in the vertical direction, the vertical gages will be
compressed. What happens to the horizontal ones? According to poisson,
with steel having a poissonâ€™sconstant of 0.3, the horizontal will expand itself
(1 mm shrink will provoke a 0.3mm expansion on the horizontal).
Thus, in this way we have two elements that increase and two elements that
decrease.
However they do not increase and decrease in the same way, right?
They have a different sensitivity two by two. Thus, we need to calculate the
sensitivity of this sensor.

This system is used on aerodynamic measurements. It is the piezoelectric
impedance head. For instance, to understand the aerodynamic
characteristics of the car, it would measure the force we need to keep the car
in place. The lower the force, the more aerodynamic. Thus, it will be the
Force divided by the velocity.
How do we measure? Instead of having two separated sensors, one for
velocity. We will have two piezoelectric materials. For the upper part you
might recognize the accelerometer of previous class. It measures
acceleration. Bellow, we have the one that will measure force. How do we
get the velocity? We need to integrate the acceleration.
